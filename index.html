Hydrogen

Quick Facts:

  • Hydrogen is the most abundent element in the universe.
  • Hydrogen is highly flammable but will not ignite unless an oxidizer (air) and ignition source are present.
  • Hydrogen is odorless, colorless, and tasteless; therefore, it is undetectable by human senses.

Copper

Quick Facts:

  • Copper is 100% recyclable and nearly 80% of the copper that has been produced is still in use today. Copper can continue to be recycled without any changes to its properties.
  • Copper can be alloyed with tin to make bronze and alloyed with zinc to make brass.
  • The average home contains 400 pounds of copper that is used for electrical wiring, pipes and appliances.

Uranium

Quick Facts:

  • The half-life of uranium-238 is 4.5 billion years. It decays into radium-226, which in turn decays into radon-222. Radon-222 becomes polonium-210, which finally decays into a stable nuclide, lead.
  • Pure uranium is a silvery metal that quickly oxidizes in air.
  • Yellowcake is solid uranium oxide. This is the form in which uranium is commonly sold before it is enriched.
